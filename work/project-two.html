AI Tool Launch Page

A dark-mode marketing site for an AI writing assistant — built to convert sceptical content teams into confident trial signups.

Timeline: 7 days · Tool: Framer · Category: AI SaaS · Dark mode · Chatbot

The problem

AI writing tools are a crowded space. The original site leaned on generic claims — "write faster", "10x your output" — that every competitor also uses. The product had a genuinely differentiated workflow feature, but it was buried in a long feature list. Visitors left without understanding what made it worth switching to.

What I built

A dark-mode Framer site that leads with the differentiator — not a feature list, but a concrete before/after demonstration of the workflow. The hero shows an interactive demo GIF. Below that: a tight value prop, social proof from content teams specifically, a Chatbase chatbot for objection handling, and a single pricing plan with a clear free-trial CTA. SEO meta, OG tags, and a sitemap included at handoff.

Key decisions

Dark mode default

Content teams work late and live in dark interfaces. Defaulting to dark mode isn't just aesthetic — it signals that this tool was built for people who take their craft seriously.

Chatbot over FAQ

A static FAQ requires the visitor to find their objection in a list. The Chatbase chatbot lets them ask exactly what they're worried about. Conversion lift on AI-tool pages tends to be higher when objections are handled conversationally.

Result

This is a concept redesign of a real AI writing tool. The build demonstrates how I approach AI-category products: lead with the differentiated workflow, reduce friction at signup, and use a chatbot to handle the "but can it actually do X?" questions that block conversion. If you're launching something in the AI space, I'd love to talk.
